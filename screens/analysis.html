#FALSESTART TO EXCELLENCE

25% of 2020 Brown Athletics award winners were on the men’s track team. That’s 25% from a single one of Brown’s thirty-eight varsity teams. Athletically, in the past six years, the members of the men’s team have set 15 school records, 110 top-10 program marks, and won too many indoor and outdoor individual Ivy League titles to count. They have had multiple second team All-Americans and produced two Olympians in the past 8 years: Evan Weinstock ‘14 and Craig Kinsley ‘12.

Brown Men’s Track and Field and Cross Country athletes have won more Academic All-Ivy awards since 2009 than anyone else on campus, except for their female teammates.









#FALSESTART TO DIVERSITY

In 2019 across the Ivy League, track was approximately 50% non-white. In percentage of black men, track outpaces all sports save for football and basketball.



Brown does not report racial data on its student athletes, so these diversity statistics are estimates based on Brown's reported team sizes and NCAA data on overall Ivy League demographics for 2019. Numbers may vary by 1-2 people due to rounding. Per this model, there were likely more black men on the Brown track team than on the soccer, baseball, lacrosse, swimming, wrestling, fencing, golf, ice hockey, tennis, water polo, rowing, and squash teams combined.

#FALSESTART TO VALUES AND FINANCIAL EFFICACY (TRACK IS A REALLY GOOD DEAL)

The operating cost per individual athlete stands out as the lowest of any sports. In the 2018-2019 academic year, each basketball player at Brown required 12 times more operating cost investment than a track and field / cross country athlete. Brown track and field / cross country athletes incurred less than 40% the median operating costs per sport.

At the program level, track and field / cross country rank 11th out of 15 sports. The program’s operating costs for 2018-2019 were less than 1/10th those of the football program.
